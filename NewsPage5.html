UPSTO Successfully Earns Prestigious UNESCO AcCreditationBy Neil Mark L. GalvezDecember 12, 2023
Soaring higher. University of Pangasinan Student Teachers Organization attended as accreditee in International
Towards sustainable student leadership excellence, University of Pangasinan Student Teachers Organization (UPSTO) paved the way as
one of the accreditee of UNESCO (United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization) Clubs Philippines on December 9—11, 2023.

Student leaders from nationwide gathered in the International Assembly of Youth for UNESCO 2023 at Manila Opera Grand Hotel in which various clubs were recognized, participated in other exciting activities, and discussed different sustainable development goals that were aligned with the program that would later contribute in helping and achieving their significant purpose by the end of 2030.

According to John Aldrin M. Balazon, UPSTO President, they were the first student organization in Phinma-UPang to be accredited by UNESCO Club Philippines. “We are given the opportunity to influence our constituents about the programs of UNESCO like quality education and lifelong learning that is aligned with our organization’s mission and vision, and of course, our own institution,” he said.

During the process, Pres. Balazon also mentioned that the process to be one of the UNESCO Clubs was very rigid, as it requires 3 levels of accreditation interview in which every step is getting further harder. He also expressed, “But one thing's for sure is that our advisers, Mr. Mahrionne Luis B. Revilla and Ms. Charmee Rae P. Velasco, are always there to support us and always remind us to stick to and uphold our organization’s mission and vision because it is the reason why we want to be accredited in the first place.”




























According to him, there were a lot of participants nationwide who wished to be one of the UNESCO Clubs, but only 82 were officially accredited. “I am proud to say that there are 3 UNESCO Clubs from Pangasinan and one of them is UPSTO,” he added with pride.

Noreen Irish S. Bautista, UPSTO Secretary, also shared her highlights from the event, expressing, “Being one of the participants in the Y4U event made me feel so honored — it was really enlightening to observe the accreditation and other activities that happened. I digested so much learnings from a variety of speakers, making every moment a pearl of wisdom. It was an unforgettable experience!”

When asked what could be improved through the process, she replied, “If there was anything that could be done better, I would like to see better information made available to everyone, myself included, on the event schedule and better communication about it. Overall, I absolutely liked the experience there and I would love to attend the next Y4U event. So, I say, let's make waves for a better world!”

John Aldrin M. Balazon, Noreen Irish S. Bautista, Lester Kenneth A. Dioquino (BSED English Representative), and their adviser, Mr. Mahrionne Luis B. Revilla, formally received the recognition as UPSTO representatives.

Looking forward to more assemblies in the future, UPSTO will promote and conduct Sustainable Development Goal #4 — a quality education and lifelong learning through various events.
