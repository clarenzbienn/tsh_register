After a long hours of playing with your friends outside, the sky started to dim and finally, your mom called you to wash up and eat the dinner that she prepared. She opened the television and the theme of 24 oras started echoing around the room. I remember asking my mom if Mike Enriquez and Mel Tiangco were married. As a response, she laughed hysterically.. What a childhood.We all grew up watching Mike Enriquez, he has been a news anchor for 24 oras for almost two decades. He is a well-respected broadcaster known for his unique voice and his iconic lines “Excuse me po!” and “Di namin kayo tatantanan!”. I used to imitate his remarkable lines when I was playing with my friends. He was part of my life for as long as I can remember. However, after a long battle with cancer, he passed away at age 71.

I remember watching Mel Tiangco delivering the news of Mike Enriquez’s death on the evening of August 29th. I felt all the emotions coming through my body and I remember my childhood days where everything was bright and colorful. It was like saying goodbye to a family member. Yet, I remembered Mike Enriquez will forever be in our hearts.

Mike Enriquez , whose real name is Miguel Enriquez was born on September 29, 1951 at Santa Ana, Manila. He is the oldest of the three siblings. Surprisingly, Mike Enriquez didn’t even plan on entering the world of broadcasting and dreamt of becoming a priest instead. According to one of his interviews, he idolizes St. Francis of Assisi, the same saint whose Pope Francis I is named after.

Mike Enriquez’s broadcasting career started in 1969 at age 19, his friend jokingly introduced him as an expected applicant, he later was asked to try since he has nothing to lose and later became a DJ for Manila Broadcasting Company. He became known for his pseudonyms “Baby Mike” and “Baby Michael”. He even had a cameo role in Andrew E’s 1992 movie Mahirap Maging Pogi as a Disc Jockey using his name baby mike.

Through broadcasting, Mike Enriquez met his wife, Lilybeth “Baby” Yumping” and got married in 1977. They had no children and adopted their dogs named Booma and Mickey, both were Mike’s nicknames.

Mike Enriquez became part of GMA in 1994 to head its radio division and even expanded it from only four stations at the time. Just like his broadcasting career, his TV career started when he least expected it. GMA network convinced Mike to be the anchor of the 1995 Philippine Senatorial Elections coverage. After that he was once again invited by the network to do a 15-minute newscast along with Karen Davila. Afterwards, he became part of multiple TV programs but is most notable for Imbestigador that earned him the nickname “Imbestigador ng Bayan” and became part of 24 oras since 2004.

At first, Mike Enriquez gives an impression of aggressive yet according to his co-workers he was far from his “imbestigador” persona. According to Marissa L. Flores, the former senior vice president of GMA Public News and Affairs,Mike is a quirky anchor and sometimes there's news that has music in it and he’ll act like he’s playing a violin. She even added that his quirkiness is beneath him and it can only be seen by the people off camera.

Another interesting fact, his tagline “excuse me po!” is because of his frequent eating of nuts, according to Jessica Soho. She also investigated who his supplier was and sometimes they would argue because of it. Soho also added that he even joked how many times he should cough and he’ll add it on (the conditions) of his contract.

Mike was known for his dedication to his work. Ahl Villanueva, the production assistant for Imbestigador recalled that even if Mike is sick, he’ll still go to work. He also shared that one time he was scolded because he didn’t text him to do a voice over because he is concerned with his health but Mike Enriquez said “I’ll tell you if I can’t (go to work). Flores recalled that Mike was the epitome of “Mr. News” because he liked news. She remembered that when the staff at the newsroom were in a frenzy because there was breaking news that needed to be delivered, he was in the middle and was ecstatic because he was so excited about the news.

His dedication to newscasting made him become one of the most prominent figures of television. Aside from his love for his profession, he’s also a well-respected man off-cam. Many of his workmates expressed their gratitude for Mike Enriquez for his kind and sweet nature.

Without Mike Enriquez, perhaps the lives of millions of Filipinos who grew up watching him will never be the same. However, his existence will never be forgotten. I’ll always admire how he didn’t fear any political figures and roasted them on national television during the 2022 Presidential Election. I remember his iconic lines. I remember how he shaped my childhood. Mike Enriquez’s unique tone will always be in my heart.
Pass The MIKE: His Legacy To Broadcasting And BeyondBy Angelo Melben B. Bautista
