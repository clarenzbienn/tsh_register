Life along the SidewalkBy Jasmine Bernadette Bembo
Running a business comes with different reasons among business-minded people. Their reasons run deeper, for money, yes, but with this money, they can provide for their family.Along the streets of AB Fernandez Avenue stands vendors that sell products ranging from bracelets, face masks, cologne, and even fruits. They stayed on the sidewalk and endured the heat and dust just to sell their products and earn their products’ worth. With the recent road construction for the Flood Mitigation Project, a few things changed in their business.

When people think of business, they think of the establishments that will be affected by the road construction along AB Fernandez Avenue, where plans for the Flood Mitigation Project have been ongoing for almost half a year.

The following paragraphs will be an insight of three individuals who are sidewalk vendors who have their business affected by the rising of the road and later on, the sidewalks. Their participation is voluntary, and their names will not be mentioned in the post for them to stay anonymous while one respondent gives their consent to post a picture but will not be named for their privacy. Loss of ProfitThey do not and will never blame the progress of the city, they even encourage it in hopes that the flood problem to stop. But there are a few things that disappointed them, mainly the change in their profit compared to when the project did not start yet.

The first interviewee stated that it does affect them greatly in profit. Being a sidewalk vendor near AB Fernandez Avenue, their current profit runs from 400 pesos a day, and the second interviewee had a profit of 4,000 pesos before the construction and it significantly changed and stayed at half profit once the construction started.

When asked if they would change their location once the sidewalk construction happen, the three respondents stated that they will remain in their location and the only thing that will change is the height of the road.

All of them hope that their past profit will come back at some point when all the road constructions are done.

Their cases are similar to the other sidewalk vendors around AB Fernandez Avenue. The charge of unloading and loading commuters and the loss of parking spaces along the side of the road are the major causes of loss of profit.

Yes, there are students from PHINMA-UPang and other campuses that walk around the sidewalk to avoid the traffic but they still barely make any more money than before.Their feelings about changeThe third respondent stated that she would never disagree with the city’s decision for progression. She had been a sidewalk vendor at the tender age of 19 years old and as she was in her seventies already, she had witnessed different kinds of change in Dagupan City.

As she has lived in the city all her life, she experienced the progression of the city and encountered different projects of the government. She does experience the impact of the Flood Mitigation Project on her business but it does not put anger in her heart rather it saddens her that she can only provide the half profit to her four grandchildren.

The hardship of being a street vendor in a progressing city is heavy. The uncertainties of their location and the uneven result of their profit continue to settle on their shoulder like a boulder, however, it does not make them stop providing for their families. Their sacrifices and hardship as a vendor do not lessen their love for their family. As long as they are alive and well, these three sidewalk vendors will never stop earning for their families.
Patience is a virtueProgression is welcome for them, they do not mind it as it will benefit them in the end once the road construction on AB Fernandez Avenue is finished. Like somehow their wish for the completion of the construction is heard, the Department of Public Works and Highways of Dagupan City announced that the deadline date of the finished road construction will be March 15, 2023, for AB Fernandez Avenue, March 30, 2023, for Arellano Street, and April 15, 2024 for MH Del Pilar Street.

Hope for the completion is near, The private sector is also on board for the project completion as this project will be one of the countermeasures of the government to the flood problem of the city.

As the sidewalk vendors wait, talking to them about the changes might ready them for the progress of the construction, they are very in favor and nothing will make them say no as it will help them because they are also being affected by the flood, they just hope that the project will left its helping hands and continue to make a progress for the betterment of the city and the relief of its citizen.
