University of Pangasinan Aids Aspiring SK Candidates Through MLOB Gabay Lakbay Program
By Quency Gylle C. CabralAugust, 2023
Fostering leadership among youth. PHINMA-UPang arranged MLOB Gabay Lakbay Program for aspiring SK Candidates on October 9, 2023.
The PHINMA University of Pangasinan took a significant stride towards fostering leadership and civic engagement among youth by hosting its first ever MLOB Gabay-Lakbay event on October 9, 2023, at the university gymnasium. The program aims to provide guidance and support to aspiring Sangguniang Kabataan (SK) candidates in the upcoming elections.The MLOB Gabay Lakbay program brought together over 300 aspiring SK candidates from various colleges and programs within the University of Pangasinan. The program was carefully designed to provide participants with insights into effective leadership, community development, and the responsibilities that come with public service.

The event kicked off with an inspiring opening ceremony featuring Mr. Adele Traspe. He emphasized the meaning of the program “Making the Lives of Others Better” and the importance of youth involvement in shaping the future of their own respective barangays.

The University of Pangasinan’s gymnasium was transformed into a hub of learning and inspiration as participants actively participated in team-building activities, group discussions, and hands-on exercises aimed at honing their leadership abilities. Topics covered included good governance, ethical leadership, and the legal aspects of being an SK official.

A notable aspect of the MLOB Gabay-Lakbay was the active participation of the Parish Pastoral Council for Responsible Voting (PPCRV). Their involvement added a layer of civic responsibility and electoral awareness to the event. PPCRV representatives conducted sessions on the importance of fair and transparent elections, encouraging the aspiring SK candidates to uphold the principles of integrity and good governance.

Father Hernan Caronongan, delivering the second talk for the program, took the opportunity to remind the participants of their responsibility towards their communities. He urged them to embody the change they wished to see in society.

The highlight of the event was the closing message delivered by Archbishop Socrates Villegas, a revered figure in the community. He underscored the sacred nature of serving the community with honesty and sincerity, proclaiming, "Ang paglilingkod sa bayan nang tapat at totoo ay paglilingkod sa Diyos" (Serving the nation with honesty and truth is serving God).

Following the sessions and workshops, an open forum provided a platform for participants to engage with the speakers, ask questions, and share their insights. This interactive segment allowed for a dynamic exchange of ideas, fostering a sense of community and collaboration among the aspiring SK candidates.

As the event concluded, participants were encouraged to apply the knowledge gained from the MLOB Gabay-Lakbay in their communities and to actively pursue their aspirations of becoming SK leaders. The program is a testament to UPang’s commitment to youth empowerment and community development, and dedication to molding lleaders with a deep sense of civic duty and spiritual integrity.
