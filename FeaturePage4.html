On-HAN(d)S: Student who have a passion that is not aligned with the taken college programBy Mark Daniel L. Barcelona
Photo from https://online.ulster.ac.uk/blog/what-is-health-psychology/
Hans Ardane C. Catabay is a 19-year-old student from Aporao, Bani, Pangasinan. He is currently in his second year of Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering at the PHINMA-University of Pangasinan (PHINMA-UPang). But unlike most of his classmates, Hans has a different passion: fashion designing.Hans grew up in a family of tailors. His grandfather made custom dresses and uniforms for students, and Hans was fascinated by the art of creating clothes. He developed a keen interest in fashion, especially in designing his own outfits. He dreamed of becoming a runway model or a fashion designer someday.

However, when it was time to choose a college program, Hans faced a dilemma. Fashion design was not a practical career option in the Philippines, especially for an only child. His parents wanted him to study at PHINMA-UPang, but the university did not offer a fashion design degree. Hans had to settle for civil engineering, a field that he was not fond of.

“It’s hard, honestly. Since mathematics is not my thing, I was stumbling around in the dark. Fortunately, I am gradually adjusting to the real nature of my degree; nonetheless, I still long for the day when I may finally follow my greatest interest, perhaps after completing engineering,” Hans said.

Despite the challenges, Hans did not give up on his passion. He continued to design and make his own clothes, using his creativity and skills. He also participated in various fashion events, such as the Lumiere pageant hosted at College of Engineering and Architecture Festival last September 2023, where he wore his own creation.

“That was my second concept that I was able to effectively bring to life, however it’s more costume-like and not the typical type of my design style,” Hans said. He described his style as “simple but elegant, with a touch of edginess.”

Hans also shared his message to other students who have a passion that is not aligned with their college program.

“Just realize that time is your ultimate ally. Never give up on the things you love, even if you can’t always get what you’ve wanted. Never underestimate the power of time and destiny; if it is meant for you, the universe will always find a way to make it happen. One day you will look up from your feet and realize that this is what you have longed for and sought,” Hans said.
Hans also expressed his hope that PHINMA-UPang would offer a fashion design program in the future.
“This is my actual dream. I often ponder myself if uPang had offered fashion design instead of civil engineering, would I have continued to pursue that course of study? You know, uPang is pretty diversified in my opinion, and if there was a fashion design degree offered here, I would love to see those who truly love fashion expand their horizons. Students would be able to reach their full potential and make a valuable contribution to the fashion industry,” Hans said.

Hans Ardane C. Catabay is a student who lives by his motto: “Always make an impact, make the Cma hallway a fashion runway.” He is an example of how one can pursue his passion, even if it is not aligned with his current college program. He is an inspiration to those who want to follow their dreams, no matter what obstacles they face.
