APSS launches Paralegal Training 2.0By Jasmine Bernadette R. BemboSeptember 6, 2023
Association of Political Science Students launches Paralegal Training 2.0: Parliamentary Procedures and Policy-Making Process on September 6, 2023.
With the theme: “Reinforcing Leadership for the Improvement of Current Student Leaders' Abilities and Knowledge” whSSC, LSC, RSO and UWO student leaders are invited to participate in the program.

Mr. Elmer D. Noriega, Assistant Dean of School of Graduate and Professional Studies of PHINMA-UPang, is the distinguished resource speaker of the program where he discusses the parliamentary process and policy making process in order for the student leaders to improve their knowledge and enhance their organizational skills.

Before the planning of the program, Mr. John Renz G. Dalaten, the governor of College of Education and Liberal Arts Student Council stated that they want and plan to consider the students that will benefit from the program and make them more professional in terms of being a student leader who served the student and as an individual themselves.

Ms. Juliana D. Dela Cruz,Chancellor of APSS stated that the benefits of the program is to equip student leaders with knowledge and skills for being a leader. “Knowing that there are processes and procedures that they have to follow, they must be knowledgeable about these matters because it is the foundation of leadership and learning agility.” She said as to further emphasize the benefits of a student leader with a knowledge of the process for being a student leader.

Ms. Dela Cruz also hoped that the Student Leaders will remember most of the topics. Everything that was talked about is crucial in the role of current Student Leaders in preparing not only themselves but the whole community for a better community with the Leaders they deserve. We learn, unlearn, and relearn.

When asked about their future plan about the program Ms. Dela Cruz said that student leaders must push forward for their legacy to continue. “Events like these should still take part in our organization's future plans and to all Flames Student Leaders and their organizations. We have covered resolution drafting, parliamentary procedures, and the policy-making process throughout the whole paralegal training for student leaders in two years. Hopefully, training with topics like these which are crucial in student leadership will continue. Paralegal Training for Student Leaders ensures the betterment of student leadership which secures the betterment of the whole studentry as well.”
