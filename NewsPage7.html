FVR library records high student visits in 2023 through computer logbook systemBy Roxan D. ResuelloOctober 22, 2023
Growing visits, expanding statistics. PHINMA-UPang Dagupan campus' logbook system stationed library records rise in visits since A.Y. 2023-2024.
PHINMA-University of Pangasinan students have set a high streak on library activity since the opening of A.Y 2023-2024, recording 17 743 visits from students, visitors, and faculty from June to October."Since the relaxation of social distancing measures, the library community has experienced a gradual resurgence, and now it can be described as fully revitalized. The library is consistently bustling with students who come in to study, complete assignments and coursework, and engage in collaborative activities.", head librarian, Ms. Armie Barbara Sabiniano, says.

In April 2023, the library launched the computer log-in system, which utilizes Google Forms to collect data on library user attendance by recording students' names, student numbers, college departments, years, and programs before entry to the facility.

The College of Engineering and Architecture (CEA) leads the highest library attendance with 4 183 visits and no less than 250 recorded students monthly. The university's Allied Health and Sciences Department (CAHS) follows second with 3 730 logs. College of Management and Accounting (CMA) comes third with 3 377. Meanwhile, 3 167 freshmen from the College of Arts and Sciences (CAS) visited the library during the first five months of their tertiary education. Students from the College of Education and Liberal Arts (CELA) record 1 207 library user attendance. 807 and 370 for the College of Information Technology Education (CITE) and College of Criminal Justice Education (CCJE), respectively.

Aside from tertiary students, the library has also welcomed students from the Senior High School Department, School of Graduate and Professional Studies, College of Law, SOG, and faculty, among others.

In an interview with Ms. Sabiniano, she says that the computer log-in system has helped the library manage itself efficiently.

"Previously, the library relied on manual logbooks for attendance, which was time-consuming and prone to errors. Transitioning to an online attendance system offers numerous benefits, including error reduction, real-time tracking, convenience, accessibility, automation, compliance, and customization. This shift streamlines operations, saves time, and enhances transparency for librarians, facilitating efficient maintenance and record-keeping of user statistics.", Ms. Sabiniano stated.

The library has also offered online services for borrowing and scanning a book, library card application, and even book journal recommendations long before the logbook system rolled out.

"The library's plan is to fully automate its services and prioritize student-centered offerings. The recently launched online services are just the beginning of this automation effort, which has yet to reach its full potential. It would be beneficial for the administration to support this library automation project by approving the acquisition of additional facilities, library systems, and databases."

When asked about future developments, such as an online seat and table reservation, Ms. Sabiniano answered that the library's chair/table-to-student ratio is insufficient and needs to be improved to cover such a project. "Once the library is adequately furnished with proper ventilation, discussion rooms, study carrels, and additional seating, online seat reservation may become necessary. However, for now, the library will remain open to all."
