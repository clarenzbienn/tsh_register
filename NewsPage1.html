Student Leaders across PHINMA Education Network attended 5th PSLS in IloiloBy Jasmine Bernadette R. BemboNovember 18, 2023
Spreading excellence across colleges. PHINMA University of Ilo-Ilo hosts PHINMA Education Leadership Summiton November 15-17, 2023
Student Leaders from nine PHINMA Education universities participated in the 5th PHINMA Education Student Leader Summit hosted by PHINMA University of Iloilo last November 15 to 17, 2023.PHINMA-University of Pangasinan, both from Dagupan and Urdaneta campuses, along with 8 other PHINMA Education schools attended the 5th PHINMA Education Student Leader Summit that was hosted by PHINMA-University of Iloilo.

With the continuation of the 5th PSLS in 2023 due to its halt because of the pandemic, 61 student leaders from PHINMA-University of Pangasinan, PHINMA-Saint Jude College in Manila, PHINMA-Republican College in Quezon City, PHINMA-Rizal College of Laguna, PHINMA-Union College of Laguna, PHINMA-Araullo University in Nueva Ecija, Southwestern University PHINMA in Cebu City, and PHINMA-Cagayan de Oro College along with PHINMA-University of Iloilo in Iloilo City attended the three-day itinerary of activities and immersive city tour from different places of Iloilo City.

The core objective of the summit is to instill in the students how to make the lives of others better, with activities that heed their active participation among peers, it is a must for the student leaders to think critically about what they can change and what must change as a group and as an individual.

In his message, Mr. Chito B. Salazar, the Chief Executive Officer of PHINMA Education, emphasized the importance of the student and labor force as a key to change. “You need to want to change the world in order to change anything.” Mr. Salazar said on the first day of the summit.

He encouraged student leaders to have the serenity to know what they cannot change, the courage to know what they can change, and the wisdom to know the difference as united students can have a powerful impact on making the lives of others better.
Mr. Christopher A. Tan, the Country Head of PHINMA Education Philippines also said in his message that students should help in creating good memories so that these memories can persist in the future. “The place God calls you to is the place where your deep gladness and the world’s deep hunger meet.” He said, highlighting that student leaders should look between the lines so that they will know where the structural injustice it and to make a move on improving the lives it affected.

When asked about his participation in the 5th PSLS, Mr. Ricardo V. Terrado Jr., the Vice President of PHINMA-UPang Supreme Student Council said that his participation as a student leader of PHINMA-UPang revealed the resonance among student leaders functioning similarly. “Creating positive pathways, whether on campus or in the community, becomes more attainable when aligned with individuals sharing identical positions and mindsets, this synergy not only aids in achieving goals but also empowers us to aim higher through critical thinking in various situations,” he said.

Terrado notes that the significant lesson he learned from the summit is “Sarili Para sa Bayan” where students have the power to change the world collectively and be the catalysts for change in our country. His shared desire to create a positive change can be manifested in collaborative efforts with the other student leaders to drive their mission and vision in taking a step forward for themselves and the betterment of of the communities they serve.

A significant lesson from the 5th PSLS was "Sarili Para sa Bayan" - the understanding that each of us has a purpose in this world and that collectively, we can be catalysts for change. Our shared desire for positive change can be realized through collaborative efforts, driving our mission and vision forward for ourselves and the communities we serve.

With the end of the Summit, a new force is created, Innovative and Integrated Student Alliance is created through the cultivation of unity among student leaders across the PHINMA Education Network from Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. IISA members of each school have advocacy to uphold and will stir change to make the lives of others better. Two student leaders from PHINMA-UPang, Mr. Terrado and Mr. Oliver B. Tumang, governor of the College of Engineering and Architecture Student Council, each advocate for Social Justice and Waste Management respectively while Mr. Robert Louise R. Gabiola, the governor of College of Arts and Sciences Student Council, is the representative for PHINMA-UPang.

As announced, in 2024, PHINMA-University of Pangasinan will host the 6th PHINMA Education Student Leaders’ Summit and also celebrate its 100th anniversary of the university.
