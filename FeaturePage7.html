Writer’s NemesisBy Shan Carlet Dacasin
Photo from https://patthomson.net/2022/03/28/tackling-writers-block/
To be a writer, one must have both the passion and courage of a great artist. One must be passionate enough to express one’s thoughts through his or her own words and brave enough to speak the truth with their works. But what is an artist without its art?An artwork is a mirror of the artist’s mind. Journalists, authors, and novelists are all writers and for me, one thing that we all have in common aside from using our words as a paintbrush; we writers suffer from “writer's block”. Every great warrior uses weapons; we writers weaponize our minds and words as our swords—like every warrior, we also have our battles. A very bloody battle every writer fights.

Writer’s block is a challenge every writer faces. A feeling of losing track of how and what can you write for your next masterpiece. Writer’s block is pure frustration and disappointment, and every writer has their own solutions to overcome it. I, myself, also encounter writer’s block and I too have my own way of overcoming it.

What works for me is that I tend to look for new inspirations and fresh motivation as writer’s block makes me feel detached from my passion for writing. To eventually help myself regain the spark in me in terms of writing: I do recreational activities, get in line with some co-writers, and spend generous amounts of time reading other’s works. The writing community is very small yet so vast in imagination, I’ve seen and met tons of great writers and I always ask them what are their ways to beat writer’s block. Some say giving their selves time to relax, others answer: fully focusing and sometimes even pressuring themselves to bring out the best in themselves—which is all good as long as it works out for them, every writer has their style and technique; that is what makes one’s work worth reading.

These are tons of things that could help us defeat mental block, and so, my fellow writers, don’t lose hope with a mere writer’s block. Remind yourself that writer’s block is nothing but a challenge and once you succeed in overcoming it, you become another and a more improved version of yourself, perceiving it as an obstacle for you to face to level up and toughen the writer in you.
