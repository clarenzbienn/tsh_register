The Ingelligence Report: Expression Is Not God’s, It’s Ours

By Gel Mendoza

I’ll be generous enough to give you time to read that again.

It seems that most people simply cannot survive without ever having something (or someone) to look up to: whether it be the presence of loved ones, life events made to be a driving force to work hard and be glorified by others later, and successes that we yearn for and would lift up to the heavens once we receive them.

We grow up to believe that we were put in this earth as a gift—to our direct families, our friends, and the society we grow up around. A gift given by a supreme being, with talents that we display that sets us apart from the rest.

But this is far from the huge mass of land, sky, and sea that was made in 6 days. Far from a world whose blueprint should supposedly be from a thick book made by unknown scriptwriters from centuries past.

For we must remember that we were born with the ability to express.
But unfortunately, it is for this reason that one of us has been falsely incarcerated.

Introductio Amadeus: On The Third Day, They Rose Again

In July of 2023, Amadeus Fernando Pagente, a drag performer more popularly known as Pura Luka Vega, performed a rock remix of “Ama Namin” at an improvised bar in Quezon City, in which clips of the performance quickly went viral. Orders for “persona non grata” (a title for someone who is unwelcome in a particular area) quickly came in for the artist from almost 20 different localities. And in October 4 that same year, they were arrested in Manila after being accused of blasphemy, but was eventually released on October 7th, 2023 after posting bail amounting to ₱72,000.

Injustice In Excelsis

Here are things I’ve conceptualized about this for the longest time:

 Religion is a choice - we were born with it, but did we even want to be surrounded by such a concept? Our family and friends have been enough to support and shape us. Not some deity we still fail to prove existed centuries ago. So with that being said, one can be free to abandon that religion once they find themselves capable of choosing for themselves.

 Religion is merely a concept - as I just said. Something we cannot see seems to know everything before it even happens. Yet, we are also told that with hard work, perseverance, and a fortunate twist of fate will lead us to anywhere we ought to be. Coincidences and day-to-day circumstances are more real than thy word made flesh.

 Separation - whoever said that we should be joined together through the grace of god when there are clearly things that are meant to be separated? Things such as people in deteriorating relationships, people who identify beyond the godly definition of man and woman and only seek to be where their truly heart is, and those whose beliefs are not necessarily needed in a particular situation, like in the case of Pura Luka Vega.

 Expression is something we’re born with - When we were infants, we only knew how to cry, make messes, break things more than fix them. But no one ever really condemned us for even the slightest mistake, because we were simply “children.” If religious people claim that everyone is a “child of god”, then wouldn’t the father be quite please with a child’s version of his teachings, knowing that he has known it by heart and is using his talents to spread that joy in the way they know best?

In the Queer Sense of the Word

Contrary to common conservative beliefs, there is an acknowledged church worldwide that recognizes and celebrates the existence of LGBTQIA+ people who choose to maintain their faith in God without having to compromise their identity. In fact, there is a branch in the Philippines, the Open Table Metropolitan Community Church located in San Juan City. And in an article written and published by the church’s head pastor, Reverend Joseph Tiu-San Jose, he gives his take on the Pura Luka Vega issue that has sparked my interest, and might even bewilder some others.

An excerpt from this article, entitled, “Jesus in Drag and Lip-Syncing” notes, “Let us be careful who and what we label as blasphemous or a heretic. We are all blasphemers and heretics from someone’s point of view no matter how devout we are. Jesus himself was nailed to the cross both as a rebel against the empire and as a blasphemer who offended religious feelings and practices…

…If Jesus is who Jesus is I don’t think he will be offended by some gay [guys] having fun in a bar lip-syncing the Lord’s prayer by a drag performer in a drag Jesus outfit and makeup more than the election of someone whose family plundered an entire nation and killed thousands of people who called them into account. Jesus would be more offended with bishops, priests, and Christian pastors who are silent with injustices and killings but who are hell-bent and will spend all effort and resources to continue the discrimination of LGBTQIA+ people.
Jesus would feel insulted with Christians praying in his name but do not care nor think about the pain, oppression, and suffering around them.” (https://opentablemcc.ph/blog/jesus-in-drag-and-lip-syncing/)

Invictus: It is I

Yes, my word is not law—it never was. My opinion platform is simply a privilege given to me to take charge of the way I so desire. After all, this exact concept is the very essence of what an opinion is.

Yes, I am not like Jesus who was able to turn water into wine or walk on the raging sea, but I know that like him, I, too have a voice. And I am determined to use it.

But to me, I know I seem to be in an image like his—someone put in this earth knowing that I can never please everyone, and has no intention of doing so. I know where I stand—in a world where one day, a coliseum might be built for me to speak my own version of the truth, and the next, I might be put back before the audience to be stoned to death.

But with everything said and done, and if it might come down to this, then so be it. I would gladly be a martyr for the truth.

And it would be my greatest honor.
