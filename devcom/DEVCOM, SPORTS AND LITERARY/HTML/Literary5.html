WHO SAID IT WAS SIMPLE?By SunflowerdaisyLet me ask you this:
Have you tired to be in our shoes as women?
Ridiculous as it could sound, but did you notice?

I hope you thought to yourself like “who said it was simple?”

To go to school or have a walk around the neighborhood while being called names by men twice our age,
Regardless of what we wear, most of us would surpass while some are captured and locked up in their cage;

Who said it was simple?

To dress as we want and not trying to impress the malicious eyes wondering around at night like some prize of a game,
To text our girl friends when we get home safe and it does not occur to us that not all of our guy friends don’t have to do the same;

Who said it was simple?

That we can’t go outside without at least bringing a pepper spray,
As daughters of most men begged us to be careful and then told our brothers to go out and play;

Who said it was simple?

To not be called a whiny oversharer as we express our feelings while men expressing theirs are considered brave,
“Stop overreacting so much” that’s what they said as it just echoes in our mind cave;

Who said it was simple?

To tell a boy that I was powerful then told me to mind my own business,
Wish I could tell them there are pretty women wallpapers that aren’t voiceless;

To do what men can do and make a beautiful art,
Yet still being belittled by them as they get a head start;

To face our monthly pains while trying to stay on top,
As men laughed at us for being “weak” nonstop;

To carry a child for nine months while being as strong and stable,
Happily Married or r@ped ‘cause life’s a gamble;

Misogyny, insensitive, gaslighting, abusive,
It’s not our fault for “triggering” those yet why put the blame on us?

Don’t tell me I can’t get mad,
‘cause being a feminist isn’t bad;

Don’t ask me what’s wrong,
For standing up and being strong;

Someday I just want people to like more than I want to change the city,
Yet some would tell me to sit still and look pretty;

Now let me ask you again; who said it was simple being a women?
Or rather, not all these scenarios are hidden yet have you tried to listen?

“Behaving” and being “quiet” is what you told us to do,
Thus you’re saying that silence is not an act of violence too?
